Childhood

Sundiata was born in around 1200 CE. He was disable at birth and couldn't walk until age 15. His mother Sogolon was the subject of ridicule among her co-wives.

His battle

After living in exile Sundiata at the age of 25, he amassed an army through alliances with nearby Mandinka people and faced Soumaoro in the battle of Kirina in 1235. Legend has it that Sundiata Keita defeated Soumaoro Kante in the battle because he was a more powerful magician than Soumaoro.

The Mali Empire

Sundiata was known as a big eater and constantly held feasts at his palace. His nickname is the "Lion King of Mali." He was the first king of the Mande people to use the title "Mansa", which meant "king of kings." He laid the foundation for a powerful and wealthy African empire and proclaimed the first charter of human rights, the Manden Charter.
